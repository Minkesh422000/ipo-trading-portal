## REIT vs. InvIT vs. Equity: Key Distinctions

| Feature | REIT | InvIT | Equity Stock |
|---------|------|-------|-------------|
| Underlying asset | Real estate (office, retail) | Infrastructure (roads, power, telecom towers, gas pipelines) | Company business |
| Listed examples (India) | EMBASSY, MINDSPACE, BIRET, NEXUS | IRB InvIT, Powergrid InvIT, IndInfravit | NSE-listed companies |
| Distribution mandate | 90% of NDCF (SEBI) | 90% of NDCF (SEBI) | No mandate |
| Typical yield (India, 2026) | 5–7% | 8–12% (higher yield, infra risk) | 1–3% dividend yield |
| Valuation metric | NAV premium/discount + yield | DCF of concession cash flows | P/E, ROCE |
| Key risk | Vacancy, tenant default | Traffic risk, tariff revision | Business risk |
| SEBI leverage cap | 49% LTV | 49% LTV | No cap |

**InvITs are NOT included in this watchlist** — they are a distinct yield-oriented asset class. Notable InvITs: IRB InvIT, Powergrid InvIT, IndInfravit Trust, NDR InvIT.

## Tax Treatment of REIT Distributions in India

REIT distributions have **four components**, each taxed differently. Most investors look only at the headline yield and underestimate the post-tax impact.

| Component | What It Is | Tax: Resident Individual | Tax: FPI |
|-----------|-----------|--------------------------|----------|
| **Debt repayment** | SPV loan repayment to Trust | **Tax-free** (return of capital) | **Tax-free** |
| **Interest pass-through** | Interest from SPV on shareholder loans | Taxable at **slab rate** | 5% withholding (treaty may reduce) |
| **Dividend from SPV** | Dividend declared by asset SPVs | Taxable at **slab rate** (post-FY22) | 20% TDS (treaty-reduced) |
| **Capital gains (unit sale)** | Profit on selling REIT units | STCG 15% (<12M), LTCG 10% (>12M, above ₹1L) | As per treaty |

**Practical impact for a 30% tax-bracket investor:**
A 6% gross distribution yield may translate to only **4.8–5.2% post-tax** depending on the component split. Indian REITs historically distribute 40–55% of income as tax-free debt repayment, making the effective tax drag lower than a pure interest instrument.

> *Each quarterly distribution notice filed on BSE Corpfiling specifies the exact split by component — always check before computing post-tax yield.*

## Office vs. Retail REIT: Comparative Framework

| Feature | Office REIT (Embassy, BIRET, Mindspace) | Retail REIT (Nexus) |
|---------|-----------------------------------------|---------------------|
| Revenue type | Fixed lease rent (95–99% of income) | Fixed rent + revenue-share (F&B, entertainment: 10–20% variable) |
| Lease structure | Area-based, 5-year terms, 5% annual escalation | Area-based + % of tenant sales (revenue share) |
| Cyclicality | Low — MNC/IT tenants have multi-year committed leases | Moderate — tied to consumer spending cycle |
| WFH risk | Present but manageable in India (RTO >80%) | Not applicable (physical retail) |
| E-commerce risk | None | Present (online substitution risk for some categories) |
| Occupancy benchmark | 90–95% is excellent | 95–98% is excellent |
| WALE benchmark | 5–8 years is strong | 4–6 years is strong |
| Cap rate | 7.5–9% | 6.5–8% (premium for retail scarcity) |
| Valuation sensitivity | Rate-sensitive (long-duration cash flows) | Consumption-sensitive |

**When to own each:**
- Office: Own when interest rates are falling (boosts NAV) + India tech hiring is healthy
- Retail: Own when India consumption growth is accelerating + new mall supply is constrained

## REITs vs. InvITs: Asset Class Clarification

InvITs (Infrastructure Investment Trusts) are often confused with REITs but are fundamentally different:

| Feature | Indian REIT | Indian InvIT |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Revenue | Lease rental (inflation-linked) | Toll/annuity/concession (often government-backed) |
| Yield (India, 2026) | 5–7% | 8–12% |
| Growth | Occupancy + rent escalation + acquisitions | Traffic growth + new concession wins |
| Key risk | Vacancy, tenant defaults | Traffic shortfall, tariff revisions, concession expiry |
| Listed examples | EMBASSY, MINDSPACE, BIRET, NEXUS | IRB InvIT, Powergrid InvIT, IndInfravit, NDR InvIT |
| Inflation protection | 5% annual lease escalation clauses | Varies by concession terms |

InvITs typically offer higher yield but carry infrastructure-specific risks. Powergrid InvIT offers near-sovereign risk (transmission tariff); IRB InvIT carries toll traffic risk. Both are valid yield instruments but require separate analysis.

## Macro Backdrop for Indian REITs

### India Grade-A Office Demand
India absorbed **70–75 msf** of Grade-A office space in FY2026 (JLL/Anarock estimates), driven by GCC expansion (Global Capability Centres — India's R&D and tech offshore hubs). India is now the world's largest GCC hub with 1,600+ centres. The GCC trend directly benefits BIRET (45% GCC tenants) and Embassy (large MNC roster).

### Return-to-Office: India vs. the World
Indian IT majors (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, Cognizant) mandate **3–5 days/week RTO**, making India's office market structurally different from the US/European office crisis. Physical occupancy in Indian Grade-A parks averages 80–85%, vs. 45–55% in US. **The US office REIT collapse thesis does NOT apply to Indian REITs.**

### Interest Rate Environment
The RBI cut rates in early FY2026, with the benchmark repo rate at ~6.0%. Further cuts are expected in FY2027. **Falling interest rates are a REIT bull catalyst** — they compress discount rates on property valuations (higher NAV) and reduce debt costs (higher distributable income). All 4 REITs have been refinancing debt at lower rates — Embassy reduced debt cost 65 bps to 7.25% in FY2026.

### FPI Activity
Foreign Portfolio Investors are the largest unitholder category in Indian REITs, particularly attracted by:
1. Tax treaty benefits on interest/dividend withholding
2. India's 6–7% REIT yields vs. 4–5% in developed markets
3. INR appreciation potential (long-term)

## India-Specific Considerations

**1. SEBI REIT Regulations (2014, amended 2021 and 2023)**
- Minimum distribution: 90% of NDCF per half-year
- Leverage cap: 49% LTV (for investment-grade rated assets)
- Under-construction assets: maximum 20% of total portfolio value
- Public float minimum: 25%
- Minimum secondary market lot: **1 unit** (SEBI 2021 amendment reduced from 200 units — dramatically increased retail participation)

**2. SEZ Benefits (Mindspace-specific)**
Tenants in SEZ parks (primarily Mindspace's Hyderabad and Pune assets) receive income tax exemption under Section 10AA of the Income Tax Act. This creates a **switching cost** — moving to non-SEZ space increases the tenant's tax burden. It's a genuine retention moat but carries policy risk if SEZ tax benefits are further phased out (already partially rationalized in previous amendments).

**3. NAV Staleness Check**
SEBI requires biannual property valuations. If the most recent NAV disclosed is more than 6 months old, treat it as potentially stale — especially in a period of rising or falling property values. Always check the valuation date when assessing NAV premium/discount.

**4. Distribution Timing**
Indian REITs typically declare distributions **quarterly**. Payment follows within 7–15 days of declaration. Check cum-distribution vs. ex-distribution date before buying — the unit price typically falls by approximately the distribution amount on ex-date.

**5. Minimum Investment**
Since the SEBI 2021 amendment, you can buy as little as **1 unit** in the secondary market. This makes Indian REITs accessible even at ₹427 per unit (Embassy).

## Monitoring Checklist

### Quarterly Triggers (Review After Each Quarterly Result)
- [ ] Committed occupancy — is it stable or improving vs. prior quarter?
- [ ] DPU declared — growth vs. prior year quarter (target: 8%+ YoY)
- [ ] NDCF per unit — is DPU payout ratio remaining at 90%+?
- [ ] Re-leasing spread — are new leases being signed above expiring lease rents? (Target: +10%+)
- [ ] LTV — has leverage increased (potential red flag) or decreased?
- [ ] New acquisitions announced — was pricing accretive (DPU-accretive) or dilutive?
- [ ] Occupancy + pipeline leasing — how much space is in LOI / advanced negotiation stage?

### Annual Review Points (At Full-Year Results)
- [ ] Update NAV per unit from the biannual independent property valuation
- [ ] Recalculate NAV premium/discount and update scores
- [ ] Review annual DPU — update distribution yield at current LTP
- [ ] Assess debt maturity profile — any large refinancing in next 12 months?
- [ ] Review sponsor pipeline — have new ROFO assets been added or dropped into the REIT?
- [ ] Monitor India macro: 10-year G-Sec yield vs. REIT distribution yield spread — if spread compresses below 50 bps, REITs are richly priced
- [ ] WFH policy updates from major tenants — TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant, MNCs
- [ ] SEBI regulatory changes affecting REIT distribution or leverage rules

### Red Flags (Immediate Review Triggers)
- Occupancy drops >2% QoQ in any single REIT
- DPU guidance cut or distribution suspended
- LTV rises above 40% (near SEBI cap)
- Sponsor distress or unit pledge enforcement
- Major tenant default (>5% of rental income)
